Chilaquiles Verdes

Description

Chilaquiles verdes is a traditional Mexican dish made from lightly fried corn tortillas covered in a flavorful salsa verde. Can be served for breakfast with a fried egg, or lunch with refried beans. Corn tortillas are drenched in a green chile sauce and commonly served at breakfast with eggs. Really great meal to make when you have more tortillas then what you can do with.

Ingredients

  • 1lb of tomatillos, husked and rinsed (about 10)
  • 3 jalapeños, stems and seeds removed
  • ½ white onion
  • water
  • ½ cup of cilantro
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • juice of ½ lime
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt

    • For the Chilaquiles

  • 12 corn torllas
  • 2 ½ tablespoons of olive oil
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ cup cotija cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped cilantro
  • 2 tablespoon of diced white onions
  • ¼ cup of sour cream or Mexican crema

Instructions

  1. Make the salsa verde by adding the tomatillos, jalapenos, and onion to a medium saucepan or pot. Fill the saucepan with enough water to cover the tops of the tomatillos.
  2. Bring water to a boil over high heat, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Move the tomatillos, jalapenos, and onion to a blender using a slottedS spoon.
  4. Add cilantro, garlic, lime juice, and salt. Blend until smootha and set aside.
  5. Stack the corn tortillas on top of each other and cut them into eighths to create small triangle wedges.
  6. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  7. Add cut tortilla wedges and saute for 10 minutes, stirring every minute or so, until the tortillas are crispy and lightly fried.
  8. Add the salsa verde and toss together to coat all the chips.
  9. Serve immediately and garnish with cotija cheese, cilantro, and Mexican crema.
